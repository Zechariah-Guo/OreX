Help & FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I buy ores?

There are two ways to buy:

  • Quick trade — Enter a quantity directly on the market card and click "Buy".
  • Detail page — Click any ore to open its detail page, enter a quantity in the Buy form, and confirm the trade.
How do I sell ores?

Same two options:

  • Quick trade — Enter a quantity on the market card and click "Sell".
  • Detail page — Open the ore's detail page and use the Sell form. The page shows how many you currently hold.

You can only sell ores you currently hold.

How often do prices change?

Prices update every 20 seconds automatically. Each update considers trends, gravity toward base price, bot activity, player trades, and random events.

What is volatility?

Volatility is a multiplier that affects how much an ore's price can change per tick. Higher volatility means bigger price swings in both directions. Coal (0.5) is the most stable, while Netherite (1.4) is the most volatile.

What are the trend indicators?

The coloured arrows on the market page show the last price movement:

  • ▲ Green = price rose on the last tick
  • ▼ Red = price fell on the last tick
  • ▶ Grey = price held steady
What is the price floor and ceiling?

Each ore has a minimum (floor) and maximum (ceiling) price. Prices cannot go below the floor or above the ceiling. When a price approaches these bounds, the algorithm nudges it back toward the centre.

Do my trades affect the market?

Yes! Your trades have a small influence on the next price tick. Buying an ore makes it slightly more likely to rise, and selling makes it slightly more likely to fall. The effect is proportional to the quantity traded.

What are market events?

Each tick, there's a 0.5% chance per ore of a market event occurring. When an event triggers, the price change for that tick is multiplied by 3×, causing a sudden spike or crash.

How do I reset my account?

Click your user icon in the navigation bar, then select Settings from the dropdown. Scroll to "Reset Account" and click it. You'll need to type your username to confirm. This restores your balance to $10,000, clears all holdings, and archives your transaction history.

What is the leaderboard based on?

The leaderboard ranks users by total portfolio value, which is your cash balance plus the current market value of all your ore holdings. It updates in real-time as prices change.

What is the average purchase price?

When you buy the same ore multiple times at different prices, your average purchase price is calculated as a weighted average. This is used to calculate your profit/loss on the portfolio page.
